How YourSay Works for Students
1
Spot a Problem
See a broken desk in your classroom? No drinking water? Dangerous road near your school? You can raise it on YourSay — anyone can submit an issue.
2
Community Votes
Once submitted, the public can vote on your issue. The more votes it gets, the higher priority it receives for funding. Your friends, family, anyone can support it.
3
See It Fixed — With Proof
When an issue is resolved, photos, videos, and reviews are uploaded publicly. Every rupee spent is shown. No guessing where the money went.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can any student raise an issue on YourSay? ▼
Yes. Any student or citizen can submit an issue. You don't need to register. Just fill in the issue title, location, category, and description. The admin team reviews it within 48 hours before it goes live for public voting.
What happens after I submit an issue? ▼
After submission, the YourSay team reviews it for validity within 48 hours. If approved, it goes live on the Issues page where the public can vote on it. High-voted issues are prioritized for fund allocation by the admin panel.
How do I know the money was actually used correctly? ▼
Every transaction on YourSay is publicly visible. When an issue is resolved, photos, videos, and a review are uploaded to the issue's page. The transaction showing how much was spent is also linked. Nothing is hidden.
Is YourSay a government website? ▼
No. YourSay is an independent public transparency platform. It is not affiliated with any government body. Our goal is to hold institutions accountable by making fund movements and issue resolutions visible to everyone.
Can I donate to a specific issue I care about? ▼
Yes — this feature is coming soon. You will be able to directly contribute to any open issue on the platform. All contributions will be tracked publicly and refunded if the issue goal is not met within 60 days.
